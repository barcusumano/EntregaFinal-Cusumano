What do I do?

Barbara Cusumano is a multi-talented individual with a diverse range of skills and experiences. After completing her studies in Audiovisual Communications at the National University of La Plata, she co-founded a small production company and film equipment rental in her hometown. She also took on the responsibility of organizing the university's film festival for seven years, a feat that she takes immense pride in.

While she enjoyed filming and producing, Barbara found that her travels exposed her to a range of odd jobs, such as farming, waitressing, cleaning, and even living in mine sites. Through these experiences, she developed a keen interest in graphic design. Over time, she honed her skills in this area, and now, after years of exploration, she is pursuing a career change by studying frontend development.

Barbara is a person who is always willing to learn and grow, and she sees this change as an opportunity to explore new horizons and take her career in a new direction. Her dedication and hard work have always been her strengths, and she is determined to apply these qualities to her new career path.

As Barbara pursues her studies in frontend development, she is excited about the opportunities that lie ahead. She is looking forward to using her skills to help others create visually stunning and user-friendly websites. Barbara is a testament to the fact that change is never too late, and it is never too late to pursue your passions and achieve your dreams.

Barbara has had a rich and diverse experience in the world of film and television production, having worked on a variety of projects ranging from music videos to short films and even producing a film festival. One of the most exciting aspects of her work has been filming music videos for popular bands such as Miranda!, La 25, and El Rey Manda.

For Barbara, working on music videos was a unique challenge because she had to capture the essence of the band's music and translate it into a visual medium. She worked closely with the bands to understand their creative vision and then used her skills as a director to bring their ideas to life on camera. This film explored the lives of a young woman with hearing loss and was praised for its raw and honest portrayal of it. Barbara was proud to be a part of this project, which was not only artistically satisfying but also had a positive impact on its audience.

This program was an important platform for young filmmakers to showcase their work to a wider audience, and Barbara was thrilled to be a part of it.

Barbara worked tirelessly to ensure that the festival was a success, and it quickly became a must-attend event for filmmakers and film lovers alike.
